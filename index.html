By day, they’re the biggest girl group in the world glittering lights, screaming fans, sold-out arenas. Their names are on every billboard. Their dances, iconic. Their songs, chart-topping. The world sees perfection. But behind the stage… lies a secret they’ve sworn to protect. By night, they become something else demon hunters. When an ancient evil rises in modern-day Seoul, threatening to consume the city in darkness, only one group stands in its way not soldiers, not superheroes, but a team of K-pop idols with mystical weapons and a bond forged in fire. Each girl brings more than just talent. One commands blades made of light. Another controls sound waves with her voice. One can open portals between dimensions. Together, they face off against shape-shifting monsters, cursed spirits, and their own haunted pasts. As their fame grows, so does the danger because the demons aren’t just hiding in the shadows anymore. They’ve started to infiltrate the world of music, fame, and power. Now, under the flashing lights of their biggest world tour, the girls must fight their greatest battle yet not just to save their fans… but to save each other. Because in this world, being an idol isn’t just about the music. It’s about surviving the darkness behind it.

K-pop Demon Hunter

“K-pop: Demon Hunter” is an upcoming animated action-fantasy film that blends the glamorous world of K-pop with dark supernatural storytelling. The film follows a fierce girl group who live double lives — dazzling fans on stage as K-pop idols by day and secretly battling demons by night. Set in modern-day Korea, the story combines vibrant visuals, magical weapons, and high-energy fight scenes with themes of sisterhood, dual identity, and female empowerment. Developed by Sony Pictures Animation and Columbia Pictures, and directed by Maggie Kang and Chris Appelhans, the movie offers a bold mix of music, magic, and myth, aiming to captivate both K-pop fans and lovers of fantasy animation.
